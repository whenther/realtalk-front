real immediate

In a normal chat app, you compose a message, then send it - basically really fast letter writing. realtalkis different. It sends each character you type, as you type it. It all happens in real time, letting you talk over each other and finish each other's sentances, - just like when you're actually talking to someone.

True, it isn't always the most effecient. But it's real conversation, and that's a rare thing for text.

real temporary

Were we're going, we don't need chat logs. You've got a box showing the last 50 characters you typed, and another showing the last 50 characters your friend typed. The server's got nothing. That's it.

In real time, a conversation moves pretty quickly. So if you want to keep up, you've got to be paying attention. Sometimes, that's not practical. But when you really want to talk to someone, needing to keep the other browser tabs closed is kind of nice.

real mobile

realtalkisn't just for desktop, and doesn't need an app. This webapp is built mobile-first, and works great on your smartphone. Since you're typing in real-time, autocorrect is disabled, so you've got nobody to blame but Freud when you "mistype" something.

make the call

The get started, create an account or sign in through webchat, Facebook, Google+, or Twitter. Under settings, you can choose a username, or leave it as your email address or Twitter handle.

Once you're logged in, check if your friends are online, or ping them via email or through the (planned) ringer extension. You can (planned) search for people by email or username, and save them as friends.

Think of connecting like making a phone call. Click on an online contact's name, and webtalk will rign them. They can choose to pick up or say they're unavailable. If they pick up, you're connected, and the chat boxes will slide in. Start typeing! To end the chat, click Quit, pick up another incoming request, or start chatting with someone else.

talking, not chatting

Once you're talking with someone, there's not much to know. Type, and your text will be sent in real-time to your friend. Hit backspace to remove a character, and Enter to clear your box. Then, have fun!

Using realtalkis different than chatting, so you shouldn't go in expecting it to do the same things. This isn't an app for multitasking. It's like talking in person; it can be done, but the person on the other end will notice. Instead, this is an app for really talking. Try it for a while, and you'll see how much give-and-take is missing for the compose, re-read, send style of chatting.

record-less. really?

Yep, really. Here's how it works: When you type a character, your browser throws away what you typed 51 characters ago, displays what you typed, and fires it off to the server. The server passes it directly to your friend without writing anything to disk. When you friend receives it, their browser throws out your 51st character too, and displays the new one. The power of modern technology lets all that happen within milliseconds, and when you press Enter or quit the chat, it's just all gone, because the text only lived on your browsers to begin with.

So on the one hand - sometimes it's nice to be able to go back and see what you were talking about. On the other hand - speak freely! Nobody's taking notes.

One thing to keep in mind, though - it's record-less, not encrypted. So, while there's nothing for hackers or the NSA to find after the fact, somebody listening to your network traffic can totally see your text. They'd get it one character at a time, so they'd have to put it all together later, but still.

the technology

realtalkuses the power of node.js and socket.io to do its work.

Nodeis a relativly new platform that runs Javascript on the server for fast, asynchronos input/output. It's perfect for applications with a lot of I/O and relativly little computation.

socket.iois a library for node that makes working with websockets, a new standard for lightweight, two-way communication between a browser and a server, easy and cross-browser.
