real immediate

In a normal chat app, you compose a message, then send it - basically really fast letter writing. realtalk. is different. It sends each character you type, as you type it. It all happens in real time, letting you talk over each other and finish each other's sentances, - just like when you're actually talking to someone.

True, it isn't always the most efficient. But it's real conversation, and that's a rare thing for text.

real temporary

Where we're going, we don't need chat logs. You've got a box showing the last 50 characters you typed, and another showing the last 50 characters your friend typed. The server's got nothing. That's it.

In real time, a conversation moves pretty quickly. So if you want to keep up, you've got to be paying attention. Sometimes, that's not practical. But when you really want to talk to someone, needing to keep the other browser tabs closed is kind of nice.

real mobile

realtalk. isn't just for desktop. This webapp is built mobile-first and works great on your smartphone, you you can grab the Android app for (planned) notifications. Since you're typing in real-time, autocorrect is disabled, so you've got nobody to blame but Freud when you "mistype" something.

make the call

To get started, create an account or sign in with your username and password.through Under settings, you can add your email and name it easier for your friends to find you. Your email also pulls a profile picture from gravatar.com for you, if you have one.

To find your friends, go to the Add Contacts page and search by username, email, or first or last name. Click Add Contact to add someone to your contacts list.

To get talking, go to the Talk page. While your on this page, you'll be marked as available to talk. You can filter your friends list, and anyone not online will be greyed out.

Think of connecting like making a phone call. Click on a contact's name, and realtalk. will ring them. They can choose to pick up or say they're unavailable. If they pick up, you're connected, and the chat boxes will slide in. Start typing! To end the chat, click Quit, pick up another incoming call, or start chatting with someone else.

talking, not chatting

Once you're talking with someone, there's not much to know. Type, and your text will be sent in real-time to your friend. Hit backspace to remove a character, and Enter to clear your box. Then, have fun!

Using realtalk. is different than chatting, so you shouldn't go in expecting it to do the same things. This isn't an app for multitasking. It's like talking in person; it can be done, but the person on the other end will notice. Instead, this is an app for really talking. Try it for a while, and you'll see how much give-and-take is missing for the compose, re-read, send style of chatting.

record-less. really?

Yep, really. Here's how it works: When you type a character, your browser throws away what you typed 51 characters ago, displays what you typed, and fires it off to the server. The server passes it directly to your friend without writing anything to disk. When you friend receives it, their browser throws out your 51st character too, and displays the new one. The power of modern technology lets all that happen within milliseconds, and when you press Enter or quit the chat, it's just all gone, because the text only lived on your browsers to begin with.

So speak freely! Nobody's taking notes.

Also, your communication with the server is encryped with SSL. That means that your username, password, and whatever weird stuff you want to talk about is protected from busy-bodys trying to listen in.

the technology

realtalk. uses the power of node.js and socket.io to do its work.

Node is a platform that runs Javascript on the server for fast, asynchronous input/output. It's perfect for applications with a lot of I/O and relativly little computation.

socket.io is a library for Node that makes two-way communication between a you and a server easy and cross-browser.
